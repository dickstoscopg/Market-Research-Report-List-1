<p><p>Unweighing gait trainers are categorized into three main types based on their operating mechanism: manual, electric, and pneumatic. Manual gait trainers rely on physical adjustments by therapists or users for support and movement control, offering simplicity and lower maintenance. Electric gait trainers use motors for powered support, allowing for more efficient movement and customizable settings, enhancing therapeutic outcomes. Pneumatic gait trainers utilize air pressure systems to provide adjustable support and buoyancy, facilitating rehabilitation through reduced impact on joints during gait training.</p></p>
