<p><p>Cogeneration systems, also known as combined heat and power (CHP) systems, can be categorized into various types based on the prime mover technology used. Steam turbines utilize steam to generate electricity, often from biomass or fossil fuels. Gas turbines operate on natural gas, offering high efficiency for large-scale applications. Fuel cells convert chemical energy directly into electricity and heat, providing a cleaner alternative. Reciprocating engines are versatile and suitable for various fuels. Additional market types may include microturbines and orbital engines, expanding the cogeneration landscape.</p></p>
