<p><p>The medical water chillers market primarily comprises two types: air-cooled water chillers and water-cooled water chillers. Air-cooled water chillers utilize ambient air to dissipate heat, making them suitable for facilities with limited space or where water resources are scarce. In contrast, water-cooled water chillers use a secondary water source to remove heat, offering higher efficiency and performance in larger installations. Each type serves critical applications in medical settings, including cooling for MRI machines, laser systems, and other sensitive equipment.</p></p>
