<p><p>The oral hypoglycemic drugs market includes several key types. Sulfonylureas (SUs) stimulate insulin secretion from pancreatic beta cells, effectively lowering blood glucose levels. Biguanides, particularly metformin, enhance insulin sensitivity and reduce hepatic glucose production. Glucosidase inhibitors slow carbohydrate absorption in the intestines, aiding in postprandial glucose control. Insulin synergists improve insulin action and can be used alongside other therapies. Each class offers distinct mechanisms and benefits, catering to diverse patient needs in managing diabetes effectively.</p></p>
