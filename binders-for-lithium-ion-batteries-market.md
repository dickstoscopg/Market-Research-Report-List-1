<p><p>The lithium-ion battery market features two primary types of binders: anode binders and cathode binders. Anode binders are used to adhere active materials to the anode substrate, enhancing electrode stability and performance during charge-discharge cycles. Cathode binders serve a similar purpose for the cathode, ensuring structural integrity and optimizing conductivity. Both binder types are crucial for maximizing the efficiency, lifespan, and overall performance of lithium-ion batteries, driving advancements in energy storage technologies across various applications.</p></p>
