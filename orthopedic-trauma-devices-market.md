<p><p>The orthopedic trauma devices market is primarily categorized into internal fixation and external fixation devices. Internal fixation devices include plates, screws, and intramedullary nails that stabilize fractures internally, promoting quicker healing and mobility. External fixation devices involve frames applied externally to support fractures, often used in severe injuries or cases where internal fixation is impractical. Both types aim to enhance patient recovery, reduce complications, and restore functionality, catering to diverse orthopedic trauma scenarios.</p></p>
