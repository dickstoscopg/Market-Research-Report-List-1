<p><p>Indirect restorative materials in the dental consumables market include all-ceramic, porcelain fused to metal (PFM), and cast-gold alloys. All-ceramic materials offer aesthetic appeal and biocompatibility, making them ideal for front teeth. PFM combines the strength of metal with the aesthetics of porcelain, suitable for both anterior and posterior teeth. Cast-gold alloys provide excellent strength and durability, often used in posterior restorations. Each type caters to specific patient needs regarding functionality, aesthetics, and longevity in dental restorations.</p></p>
