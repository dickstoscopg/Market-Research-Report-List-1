<p><p>Diabetic Foot Ulcers (DFUs) and Pressure Ulcers are common complications associated with diabetes and prolonged pressure, respectively. DFUs are open sores or wounds that occur on the feet of diabetic patients due to reduced blood flow and nerve damage. They can lead to severe infections and, in extreme cases, amputations. Pressure ulcers, also known as bedsores, develop when sustained pressure on the skin impairs blood flow, typically in individuals with limited mobility. Both conditions require effective management and treatment, creating a significant demand for medical products and therapies.</p><p>The Diabetic Foot Ulcers and Pressure Ulcers Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 8.2% during the forecast period, driven by an increasing global prevalence of diabetes, an aging population, and rising awareness of wound care management. Furthermore, the expanding healthcare infrastructure and better reimbursement policies for wound management services are enhancing access to effective treatment options.
