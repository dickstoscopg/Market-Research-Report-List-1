<p><p>Single-wall carbon nanotubes (SWCNTs) are cylindrical nanostructures made of a single layer of carbon atoms arranged in a hexagonal lattice. The unique characteristics of SWCNTs, such as high tensile strength and electrical conductivity, make them ideal for use in advanced composite materials, nanoelectronics, and sensing devices.</p><p>The market for single-wall carbon nanotubes is anticipated to witness substantial growth, driven by increasing demand in sectors such as electronics, automotive, and healthcare.
